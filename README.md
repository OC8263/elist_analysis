# Company Background

Founded in 2018, Elist is an e-commerce company specializing in popular electronics and serving a global customer base. Like most online retailers, it sells products through both its website and mobile app. The company uses a range of marketing channels, including email campaigns, SEO, and affiliate partnerships. In recent years, its top-selling items have included products from Apple, Samsung, and ThinkPad.

# Executive Summary

From 2019–2022, the business generated strong revenue growth driven primarily by premium product demand in North America. While the company maintained a solid product mix and improved refund performance, recent trends indicate declining order values and increased dependence on lower-spend transactions, creating opportunities to refine pricing and loyalty strategies.

• From 2019–2022, the business generated $28.1M in sales across 108K orders, with an average order value (AOV) of $260 

• North America was the strongest-performing region, driven by premium products such as 27" 4K gaming monitors, Apple AirPods, and MacBook Air, while LATAM lagged with lower and more evenly distributed revenue 

• Product performance was volatile, peaking in December 2020 with strong gaming monitor sales, while some products such as Bose Soundsport headphones weakened significantly by late 2022 

• The loyalty program contributed $11.0M in sales but had a lower AOV ($240) compared to non-members, who generated $17.1M with a higher AOV of $275 

• Refund rates for key Apple products improved substantially over time, reaching 0% by 2022, indicating stronger product quality and customer satisfaction 

Despite strong overall revenue and a healthy premium product mix, declining order values and increased reliance on lower-spend transactions highlight the need to optimize pricing, product mix, and loyalty strategy to sustain profitable growth

# Deep-dive Insights
Overall performance shows a strong growth phase through 2020 followed by a sustained decline across 2021–2022. The downturn is primarily driven by reduced order volume rather than pricing pressure, as AOV remained relatively stable. Growth rate analysis indicates a sharp spike in 2020, followed by negative year-over-year trends, reinforcing the view of demand normalization after an exceptional peak period.

Total sales increased significantly from 2019 to 2020, supported by both higher order volumes and rising AOV. Monthly growth rates peaked during 2020, with consistent positive momentum throughout the year.

From 2021 onward, sales entered a declining phase:

	• Year-over-year growth turned negative 
	• Order growth dropped sharply, becoming the primary driver of declining revenue 
	• AOV showed only a gradual decrease, indicating relatively stable pricing and customer spend per transaction 
	
This pattern suggests that the business is experiencing a volume-driven slowdown, likely due to reduced customer demand rather than changes in pricing or product value.

## Reginal Trends

<img width="1932" height="430" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/565c3dc4-3292-4636-834a-92f45ce14775" />


	Revenue performance varies notably by region, with North America significantly outperforming other markets due to stronger demand for premium electronics.
	
	• North America generated the highest revenue by a wide margin, led by strong sales of the 27" 4K gaming monitor, Apple AirPods, and MacBook Air 
	• EMEA showed a similar premium product mix, though revenue levels were lower and more evenly distributed across products 
	• APAC demonstrated a more balanced sales composition, with relatively stronger MacBook Air performance and lower dependence on AirPods
	• LATAM recorded the lowest overall revenue, with sales spread more evenly across products and a higher contribution from mid-range items 
	• Despite regional differences in scale, premium electronics remained the primary revenue drivers across all markets 
	• Regional demand patterns suggest opportunities for targeted product positioning and localized pricing strategies


## Loyalty Program Insights

<img width="1594" height="734" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/db73b5b9-4d01-44cd-ad93-feb75a0494c3" />


	The loyalty program has become a major revenue driver over time, although lower member spending per transaction suggests opportunities to improve profitability.
	
	• The loyalty program’s share of total sales increased significantly from 10.75% in 2019 to 54.84% in 2022 
	• Growth in member contribution indicates strong program adoption and an expanding base of repeat customers 
	• Loyalty members likely drive higher engagement and purchase frequency, contributing substantially to overall revenue growth 
	• Despite strong revenue contribution, members recorded a lower average order value ($240) compared to non-members ($275) 
	• Lower member AOV may indicate margin pressure from discounts, rewards, or promotional incentives within the program 
	• Current trends suggest opportunities to optimize the loyalty strategy by increasing member spend and improving overall profitability





## Refund Trends

<img width="1926" height="456" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/03f9c812-dc80-44b9-859f-3b75e70cae58" />



	Refund performance improved significantly over time, particularly for Apple products, although historical spikes highlight opportunities to further reduce returns for premium devices.
	
	• From 2019–2023, the overall refund rate averaged 4.97%, with more than 5,300 units refunded 
	• Apple products, particularly MacBook Air and AirPods, accounted for the highest refund volumes and rates 
	• The MacBook Air recorded the highest refund rate at 18.31% in 2019, while AirPods peaked at 16.86% in 2021 
	• Refund rates for all Apple products declined to 0% by 2022, suggesting major improvements in product quality, fulfillment, or customer satisfaction 
	• Non-Apple products performed more consistently, with relatively low refund rates, including Samsung charging cables (2.36%) and Bose headphones (0%) 
	• Despite recent improvements, earlier refund spikes indicate continued opportunities to strengthen quality control and post-purchase support for high-value products
	

## Recommendations


	• Increase AOV 
		○ Introduce bundling (e.g., accessories with premium devices) 
		○ Set spend thresholds for promotions (e.g., “Spend $300+, get reward”) 
	• Optimize loyalty program 
		○ Reduce blanket discounts; focus on value-based rewards 
		○ Incentivize higher basket sizes rather than repeat low-value purchases 
	• Double down on premium products 
		○ Expand and promote top performers (e.g., monitors, laptops) 
		○ Prioritize North America while identifying premium upsell opportunities in LATAM 
	• Address product decline 
		○ Reassess underperforming items (e.g., Bose Soundsport headphones) 
		○ Refresh assortment or adjust pricing strategy 
	• Sustain low refund rates 
		○ Maintain quality control and customer experience improvements 
		○ Leverage this as a trust signal in marketing 
	• Focus on profitable growth
