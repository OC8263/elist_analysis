# Company Background

Founded in 2018, Elist is an e-commerce company specializing in popular electronics and serving a global customer base. Like most online retailers, it sells products through both its website and mobile app. The company uses a range of marketing channels, including email campaigns, SEO, and affiliate partnerships. In recent years, its top-selling items have included products from Apple, Samsung, and ThinkPad.

# Executive Summary

Total sales grew steadily through 2019 and accelerated sharply in 2020, peaking in December 2020 at over $646K with approximately 2,055 orders. This growth was driven by a strong increase in order volume, supported by a rise in average order value (AOV), which reached its highest levels during the same period.
The surge in 2020 aligns with exceptional demand in electronic, particularly laptops and related products, consistent with increased work-from-home and home entertainment needs during the COVID-19 period.
Following this peak, sales declined through 2021 and 2022, with total sales down year-over-year, primarily due to a reduction in order volume. AOV showed only a modest decline and remained relatively stable at approximately $260 per order, indicating that the downturn was driven more by weakening demand than pricing pressure.
Product-level insights show that revenue remains concentrated in a small number of high-performing electronics products, with laptops and gaming devices contributing the largest share of sales. This highlights both a strength in core categories and a potential concentration risk.
Overall, the data suggests that the 2020 performance spike was temporary, followed by a normalization phase. The current challenge is to rebuild order volume.

# Deep-dive Insights
Overall performance shows a strong growth phase through 2020 followed by a sustained decline across 2021–2022. The downturn is primarily driven by reduced order volume rather than pricing pressure, as AOV remained relatively stable. Growth rate analysis indicates a sharp spike in 2020, followed by negative year-over-year trends, reinforcing the view of demand normalization after an exceptional peak period.

Total sales increased significantly from 2019 to 2020, supported by both higher order volumes and rising AOV. Monthly growth rates peaked during 2020, with consistent positive momentum throughout the year.
From 2021 onward, sales entered a declining phase:
	• Year-over-year growth turned negative 
	• Order growth dropped sharply, becoming the primary driver of declining revenue 
	• AOV showed only a gradual decrease, indicating relatively stable pricing and customer spend per transaction 
This pattern suggests that the business is experiencing a volume-driven slowdown, likely due to reduced customer demand rather than changes in pricing or product value.

## Product Trends

<img width="1934" height="522" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/47abb923-683f-49df-8854-5e5449883973" />




Revenue is consistently dominated by a small group of high-performing electronics—particularly premium monitors, wireless audio, and laptops. The 27" 4K gaming monitor leads throughout most periods, with Apple AirPods and MacBook Air close behind.
Demand patterns show pronounced fluctuations over time rather than steady growth. There’s a noticeable surge across top products during the 2020–2021 period, likely tied to remote work and at-home setup demand. Following that peak, revenue softens and becomes more inconsistent, suggesting a normalization phase after unusually high demand.
Lower-tier products—such as accessories and smaller electronics—remain relatively flat and contribute only marginally to overall revenue. Their trends show minor seasonal variation but no meaningful upward momentum.

Revenue patterns vary significantly across regions, but the overall hierarchy of top-performing products remains consistent.	
North America leads by a wide margin, with strong demand for premium products—27" 4K gaming monitor, Apple AirPods, and MacBook Air dominating.
EMEA shows a similar product mix but at lower, more evenly distributed sales levels.
APAC has a more balanced split, with relatively stronger laptop (MacBook Air) performance and less reliance on AirPods.
LATAM generates the lowest revenue, with smaller, more evenly spread contributions and relatively higher share from mid-range products.


## Loyalty Program

<img width="1944" height="462" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/68bc60f4-77c2-49c3-9c54-cfd3022edd69" />


The loyalty program has evolved from a minor contributor to a central driver of the business, growing its share of total sales from 10.75% in 2019 to 54.84% in 2022. This indicates strong adoption and suggests that members are becoming the core repeat customer base, likely contributing through higher engagement and purchase frequency over time. However, while the program successfully drives over half of total revenue, members exhibit a lower average order value ($240 vs. $275 for non-members), which may indicate margin pressure due to discounts or reward structures. Overall, the program is effective in building customer loyalty and revenue share, but there is a clear opportunity to optimize it by increasing member spend and improving profitability.


## Refund rates

<img width="1944" height="452" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/bbb79dff-1fa6-4599-b9d4-e172693d6f08" />

<img width="1942" height="444" alt="image" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/c1e532a5-6f1f-4135-935f-fa16848e0b06" />



From 2019 to 2023, the overall refund rate across all products averaged 4.97%, with over 5,300 units refunded. Apple products, particularly MacBook Air and AirPods, drove the majority of refund volume and exhibited the highest rates—MacBook Air peaked at 18.31% in 2019, and AirPods reached 16.86% in 2021. However, refund rates for all Apple products dropped to 0% in 2022, indicating a significant improvement in product quality or fulfillment processes. Non-Apple items like Samsung charging cables (2.36%) and Bose headphones (0%) performed well. While the recent trend is positive, the historical spikes suggest ongoing opportunities to reduce returns through better quality control and post-purchase support, especially for high-AOV Apple devices.
